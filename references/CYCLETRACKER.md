## Phase Reference Guide

### Phase 1: Menstruation (Day 1–5 approx.)

**What's happening:** Estrogen and progesterone are both low. Prostaglandins cause uterine contractions and may cause cramping, fatigue, lower back ache.

**Training:**
- Day 1–2: Honor your energy. Light movement, walking, mobility work is fine.
- Day 3–5: Most women feel energy returning. Resume normal training.
- Iron is being lost — prioritize iron-rich meals.

**Nutrition:**
- Iron-rich foods: red meat, lentils, fortified cereals, spinach
- Pair iron with vitamin C (lemon juice, bell pepper) for absorption
- Anti-inflammatory foods: berries, turmeric, omega-3s, ginger
- Avoid excess caffeine Day 1–2 (can worsen cramps)
- Maintain protein targets

**Feel:** Variable. Don't judge fitness on Day 1 performance.

---

### Phase 2: Follicular (Day 6 → Ovulation, ~Day 14)

**What's happening:** Estrogen is rising steadily. This is your performance window. You are closer to male physiology in this phase than any other time. Insulin sensitivity is high, recovery is faster, pain tolerance is higher, mood tends to be elevated.

**Training:**
- Push harder in this phase
- Best time for PR attempts, heavy lifts, high-intensity intervals
- Neuromotor performance is at its best — good for Olympic lift variations (hang clean, snatch)
- Volume can be higher
- Recovery is faster — can handle more training stress

**Nutrition:**
- Carb-forward: body uses glucose efficiently
- Stick to base macro targets
- Pre-workout fueling is especially important to maximize the performance window
- Great time to test race-day nutrition protocols

**Feel:** Strong, motivated, energetic. This is your biological "green light" phase.

---

### Phase 3: Ovulation (Around Day 14, varies)

**What's happening:** LH surge triggers egg release. Estrogen peaks briefly, then begins to fall as progesterone rises.

**Training:**
- Peak power output possible for 1–2 days around ovulation
- Injury risk increases — estrogen peak causes ligament laxity (especially ACL)
- Be mindful on technical movements and landing mechanics
- Thorough warm-up is non-negotiable

**Nutrition:**
- Keep carbs moderate-high
- Zinc-rich foods support ovulatory health: pumpkin seeds, beef, oysters

**Feel:** Often a brief energy peak. Can feel slightly warm (LH surge raises body temp slightly).

---

### Phase 4: Luteal (Day 15 → Day 28 approx.)

**What's happening:** Progesterone dominates. Resting core temperature rises ~0.5°C. RPE for the same effort feels higher. Body shifts to burning more fat and protein (less carbs). Brain serotonin drops in late luteal → mood changes, cravings, fatigue. PMS symptoms are progesterone-related.

**Training:**
- Maintain loads, reduce volume if needed
- Don't attempt PRs — perceived effort is inflated
- Strength is actually still accessible — the issue is feel, not capacity
- Good time for technique work, hypertrophy in moderate rep ranges (8–12)
- Running: pace may feel harder at same heart rate — use RPE, not pace/HR
- Heat tolerance is reduced (elevated core temp) — hydrate extra on hot days

**Nutrition:**
- Increase protein to 140–150g/day (progesterone accelerates protein breakdown)
- Increase healthy fats: avocado, olive oil, nuts, fatty fish
- Carbs moderate — cravings are real and hormonal; plan for satisfying carbs rather than fighting them (sweet potato, oats, dark chocolate)
- Magnesium can help with PMS symptoms and sleep (200–400mg glycinate form)
- Extra electrolytes — aldosterone increases sodium excretion
- Do not restrict calories here. Body is working hard and needs fuel.

**Feel:** May feel heavier (2–5 lbs water retention is normal — do not weigh on Day 22–26 and compare to Day 10). Fatigue, moodiness, cravings are all real physiological events, not weakness.

**Weight note:** Luteal phase weight gain is NOT fat. It's water driven by progesterone and aldosterone. Weight will drop 2–5 lbs at the start of your period naturally.

## No Cycle Yet (While Breastfeeding)

Prolactin suppresses the HPG axis — this is normal and expected. It does NOT mean your hormones are flat. Progesterone and estrogen still fluctuate.

Without a cycle, use these proxies:
- Energy is high, mood is good, sleep is decent → treat like follicular phase
- Fatigue, cravings, bloating, mood dip → treat like luteal phase

Track your energy/mood 1–10 daily. Patterns will emerge.

Log here when your cycle returns. Most women breastfeeding exclusively see return between 6–18 months postpartum.
