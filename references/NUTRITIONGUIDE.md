## Race Day Protocol

Race days require a specific nutrition strategy to prevent GI issues during hard effort.

**Macros:** 150P / 195C / 50F  
Fat is lower on race days — fat slows gastric emptying and causes GI distress during high intensity running.

### Race Day Meal Timing

**Morning (normal fat okay — 8+ hours before race):**
- 2 eggs + white sourdough + fruit + small amount butter
- Keep it simple, familiar, no new foods

**Lunch (4–6 hours before gun — LOW FAT, COOKED ONLY):**
- Lean protein (chicken, turkey, tuna) + white rice or white bread + cooked vegetables
- No raw salad, no avocado, no cheese, no olive oil, no high fiber foods
- Target: under 8g fat

**Afternoon snack (2–3 hours before gun):**
- Chobani protein drink + rice cakes or plain crackers or fruit
- Avoid whey protein (Premier Protein, Fairlife) — dairy whey can cause GI distress during hard effort
- Target: under 5g fat

**Pre-race fuel (90 min before gun):**
- Banana OR 3 dates — nothing else
- Zero fat, zero fiber, pure carbs only

**Post-race recovery meal:**
- Fat comes back — normal eating resumes
- Priority: protein + carbs within 30–45 min of finishing
- Milk supply may dip after hard effort — eat and hydrate aggressively for 24 hours

### GI Trigger Foods — AVOID after noon on race days
- Raw salad or raw vegetables
- Avocado
- Any cheese
- Olive oil or any added fat
- Whey protein products (Premier Protein, Fairlife)
- High fiber foods (beans, lentils, bran)
- Anything new or untested

## Daily Nutrition Structure

**5 AM — Pre-workout (mandatory every lifting day)**  
Why: Fasted morning training spikes cortisol more in women. Blunts adaptation and chips away at milk supply over time.
- Lifting days: Chobani protein drink — 20P | 14C | 3F | 170 cal
- Deload/easy days: Banana — 0P | 27C | 0F | 105 cal

**Post-workout (within 30 min)**  
Why: Muscle protein synthesis window. Blunts cortisol, kickstarts recovery.  
What: ~25g protein + ~25g carbs

**Breakfast**  
Real food. ~20–25g protein | ~30–40g carbs | moderate fat
- Eggs + sourdough + fruit
- Eggs + cottage cheese + fruit
- Pancakes with protein additions

**Pre-run (60–90 min before)**  
Simple carbs + small protein. No fat, no fiber.
- Banana
- 2–3 dates
- Rice pudding (homemade — also baby safe)

**Post-run (within 30 min)**  
25g protein + 30g carbs

**Dinner**  
30–40g protein + vegetables + moderate carbs. Keep fat moderate.

**Late snack (if needed to close gaps)**  
Protein-forward — Chobani, Fairlife, Greek yogurt, kefir + cereal

## Protein — The Non-Negotiable

Target: 150g daily — never below this

**Best sources:**
- Eggs (6g each)
- Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, kefir
- Chicken, turkey, lean beef, flank steak
- Salmon and fatty fish
- Chobani protein drink (20g), Fairlife (30g), Premier Protein (30g)
- Lentils + edamame

**Easy protein targets by meal:**

| Meal | Target |
|---|---|
| Pre-workout | 15–20g |
| Post-workout | 25g |
| Breakfast | 20–25g |
| Lunch | 25–35g |
| Dinner | 30–40g |
| Late snack | 15–20g if needed |

## Carbohydrate Timing

Don't restrict carbs. Especially not while breastfeeding and racing.

| Timing | Approach |
|---|---|
| Pre-workout | Yes — simple carbs |
| Post-workout | Yes — moderate carbs |
| Pre-run/race | Yes — simple carbs, low fiber |
| Lunch/dinner | Yes — rice, sourdough, fruit, sweet potato |
| Evening snack | Moderate — close gaps as needed |

Higher carb days: Race days (195g), trail run days (175g)  
Lower carb days: Rest days (165g, never below 100g)

## Fat Target

65g regular/training/run days. 50g race days.

**Best sources:**
- Eggs (yolks)
- Butter (small amounts)
- Salmon and fatty fish
- Cheese (Havarti, panela, mozzarella)
- Avocado
- Olives and olive oil
- Full fat dairy (kefir, milk, yogurt)

Race day: Keep fat in morning only. Near zero after noon until post-race.

| Tart cherries | Post-race/post-lift | Anti-inflammatory |

## Hydration Protocol

- Daily minimum: 90–100 oz (3 liters)
- Training days: Add 16–24 oz around session
- Race days: Electrolytes from the start — don't wait
- Electrolytes: Sodium is key — LMNT, Liquid IV, or salt + citrus
- Milk supply cue: Dark yellow urine by 10 AM = drink more

## Cycle-Based Nutrition Adjustments

### Menstruation (Day 1–5)
- Iron-rich foods priority — red meat, lentils, spinach
- Pair iron with vitamin C for absorption
- Anti-inflammatory: berries, turmeric, omega-3s
- Honor energy on Day 1–2, resume training Day 3–4
- Don't restrict calories — body is working hard

### Follicular (Day 6 → Ovulation ~Day 14)
- Best performance window — push hard
- Carb-forward, body uses glucose efficiently
- Protein: 150g, Fat: 65g, Carbs: standard targets
- Best time for PR attempts and race efforts

### Ovulation (Around Day 14)
- Peak power for 1–2 days
- Watch injury risk — ligament laxity peaks
- Zinc-rich foods: pumpkin seeds, beef

### Luteal (Day 15 → Day 28)
- Increase protein to 155–160g — progesterone accelerates breakdown
- Increase healthy fats slightly
- Plan satisfying carbs — sweet potato, oats, dark chocolate
- Extra magnesium and electrolytes
- Expect 2–5 lbs water retention — NOT fat
- Do not restrict calories

## Breastfeeding Red Lines

Signs of underfueling:
- Noticeable drop in milk output
- Extreme fatigue beyond normal training
- Mood instability
- Slower workout recovery
- Increasing hair loss

**If any appear:** Increase carbs by 20–30g first. Do not cut anything. Do not investigate other causes before eating more.
